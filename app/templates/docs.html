I. Elucidating AI


What is AI?

At the convergence of computers, math, and data — there is AI; a computer program that aims to mimic the human decision making process.

Picture AI as an umbrella — under this umbrella there is; Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and Reinforcement Learning.

What type of AI is Dojo?

The proprietary Dojo algorithm creates both machine learning and deep learning models.

The algorithm automatically recognizes the type of task, and creates the proper model for optimal accuracy. Depending on the data, the algorithm may utilize an ensemble of AI's.

What are examples of AI?

AI is an inherent part of our everyday lives — whether we’re aware or not.

From Netflix to the Instagram explore page, all the recommendations and pictures on our feed, this is AI at work.

AI exists in our homes; smart devices that react to a change in temperature or lighting

II. Behind The Scenes


How does AI learn?

Using math, of course! AI models have different mathematical functions for different tasks.

The model learns by minimizing the error of that function. The "learned" formulas leading to minimal loss is how the model makes predictions on new, un-seen data.

Types of ML & DL

ML is a subset of AI that uses statistical learning algorithms to build smart systems. DL is a subset of AI that uses neural networks to mimic the way a human brain filters information.

The two most prevalent AI tasks are Regression, and Classification.

Classification task: predict if AAPL stock will go up or down tomorrow. Regression task: predict the close price of AAPL stock tomorrow

What is AI capable of?

Human neural network— “Number of neurons ~ 86 Billion, number of synapses ~ 150 Trillion, another generalization: average number of synapses per neuron ~ 1,744.”

Specialized artificial neural network— “GPT-3’s full version has a capacity of 175 billion machine learning parameters.”

150 trillion/150 billion = 1,000. The most powerful AIs would have to scale by a factor of one thousand for their connections to be on par with the brain…

III. Dojo


Why Dojo?

Thanks to Dojo, you no longer need to hire an engineer to develop AI models. Rapidly create your own AI, for any task you'd like.

Accuracy: A custom model will not outperform Dojo. The algorithm continually outperforms custom models.

How is accuracy calculated?

20% of your data is used to test the model accuracy

Classification: The accuracy is calculated by dividing the amount of correct predictions, by the total number of samples.

Regression: The accuracy is calculated using the R2 score. 1 - (sum of squares of misses/total sum of squares)
