Dojo is democratizing access to artificial intelligence via the ethereum blockchain.

When an NFT is minted (crypto-punks, for example), the image is encoded to bytes & stored on chain. The same can be done with an AI model.

  • Dojo has developed a proprietary algorithm which enables anyone to create AI, with no code.
  • Once created, the AI is encoded to bytes & minted as an NFT to the creator.
  • You can then query the AI for use at any time, or sell it on the Dojo marketplace.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What happens to my data?

    Your data is used to train the AI model. After it is trained, the data is discarded entirely. Your data will never be stored on our servers.

  • How does Dojo make money?

    Training your AI model is free. Due to the scarcity of computational resources, this is incredibly generous. Once created, you will have the option to either mint or destroy the model. If the model is accurate, and you decide to mint it, Dojo will take a fee. Any models bought & sold on the marketplace also incur a fee. However, if you decide to destroy the model, it will cost you nothing.

  • What types of data are currently supported?

    As of right now, Dojo only supports tabular data. This includes CSV, HTML, and XLSX files. The algorithm automatically recognizes the type of task it is (Classification, Regression, Clustering, etc...), based on the data, and trains the proper model. Development of computer vision and document processing models are already underway, and will soon be available for beta.

  • How does the algorithm work?

    For you technologically inclined folks, Dojo is an extremely powerful Auto-ML solution. As stated above, the algorithm automatically recognizes the task based on the uploaded data. From there, the algorithm does everything a human engineer does when building a model. This includes EDA, feature selection/encoding/scaling, model ensembling, hyper-param tuning, and more. A custom model will not out-perform the Dojo Auto-ML solution.

  • How does the platform work?

    AI models are minted as NFTs. Let me break that down. Since AI models can be quite large, like GBs in size, only a reference to the model is stored on chain. More specifically, a struct is stored on chain with the following properties: Name, Description, Accuracy. The actual model bytes are compressed and stored in a database.
